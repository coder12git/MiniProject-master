Summary


Foam packaging is a commonly used material for cushioning and protection of fragile items during shipping and handling. However, it has a relatively low level of sustainability.


Foam packaging is typically made from synthetic materials such as polystyrene or polyethylene. While it provides excellent shock absorption and insulation properties, it is not biodegradable and has a significant environmental impact.


Efforts to improve the sustainability of foam packaging include the development of bio-based foams and recycling programs. However, alternatives such as biodegradable or recyclable materials may be more environmentally friendly options.
